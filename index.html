S&P 500 Subsidiary Network
Who owns what in corporate America? Capital Map US maps every disclosed subsidiary of every S&P 500 company — parsed directly from SEC EDGAR Exhibit 21 (10-K annual filings). Explore the hidden ownership web across 11 GICS sectors, from mega-conglomerates to lean pure-plays.
503 S&P 500 Parents
9,690 Subsidiaries
9,846 Relationships
11 GICS Sectors
Data Source

All subsidiary data is extracted from SEC EDGAR Exhibit 21 filings — the legal attachment to each company's 10-K annual report that lists significant subsidiaries and their jurisdictions of incorporation.

Updated: 2026-06-14  |  Source: SEC EDGAR / EDGAR Full-Text Search

How to Use

NETWORK — Force-directed graph showing all 503 parent companies and 9,690 subsidiaries. Click any parent node to highlight its subsidiary network. Use the legend to filter by GICS sector. Scroll to zoom, drag to pan.

BY SECTOR — Cards for all 11 GICS sectors. See parent count, total subsidiaries, and the top 5 companies by subsidiary count per sector. Click a card to switch to the Network view filtered to that sector.

TABLE — Sortable, searchable table of all 503 parent companies. Sort by ticker, name, sector, or subsidiary count. Search by ticker or company name.

Limitations
  • 6 S&P 500 companies embed Exhibit 21 within the 10-K body rather than as a separate attachment and are excluded from this dataset: ANET ERIE FDXF ODFL VRSN VRSK
  • Subsidiary data reflects SEC disclosure at the time of each company's most recent 10-K filing. Acquisitions, divestitures, or restructurings after filing are not reflected.
  • Only subsidiaries explicitly listed in Exhibit 21 are included. Minority stakes and joint ventures may be omitted at the company's discretion under SEC rules.
  • Jurisdictions are as disclosed — many filings omit jurisdiction information for individual subsidiaries.
